Thesis

MS(R) Thesis

Title: Design and Analysis of BLDC Motor for UAV Applications

  • Designed and analyzed outrunner radial flux BLDC (ORRFBLDC) motors for aerospace applications, focusing on their integration into unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) and addressing challenges specific to single-rotor helicopters, such as f ixed-speed operation and mechanical load management.
  • Utilized a comprehensive design methodology, including analytical calculations and finite element analysis (FEA) through magnetostatic, transient, dynamic, and parametric simulations.
  • Developed prototypes and performed laboratory testing to evaluate motor suitability for aerospace applications.
  • Conducted electromagnetic FEA of commercially available BLDC motors using ANSYS Maxwell, incorporating inputs for core loss determination and performance optimization.

PhD Thesis

Title: Development and Control of Actuators for Roll Stabilization in Dual-Spin Artillery Projectile

  • Designed a dual-purpose fuze model utilizing a permanent magnet (PM) generator for both power generation and roll control, offering a cost-effective solution for precision munitions.
  • Investigated resistive braking methods for roll correction by digitally varying resistance to produce variable braking torque, while addressing challenges in reliability and compactness.
  • Developed a mathematical model for an eddy current brake that accurately repre sents high-speed behavior and estimates braking torque based on excitation current and operating speed, incorporating temperature effects due to the varying conductivity of the braking element.
  • Explored magnetorheological (MR) fluid as a smart material for compact, energy efficient braking, utilizing a hybrid configuration to optimize braking torque at high angular speeds. Modeled the MR brake to enhance the average magnetic field in the fluid gap, ensuring robust operation and simple assembly for high-speed dual-spin applications.
